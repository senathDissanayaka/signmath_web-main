ABOUT THE SYSTEM

This system is specially designed to support deaf and mute students in learning essential topics such as shapes, currency, and basic mathematics. It focuses on providing a visually interactive and inclusive learning environment where students can grasp concepts using sign language and animated visuals. By removing the reliance on spoken instructions, the platform helps learners gain confidence and independence. The system’s user-friendly design encourages self-learning and caters to the unique needs of students with hearing and speech impairments. This makes it a valuable tool for promoting equal access to education for differently-abled learners.

KEY FEATURES

The system includes three main learning modules tailored for deaf and mute students. The shape module introduces common geometric shapes using animated visuals and sign language explanations. The currency module teaches recognition of coins and notes, along with real-life usage. The math module helps students practice basic operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with the support of visual tools and signs. Each module is designed to be interactive, easy to understand, and suitable for self-paced learning. These features together make the system highly accessible and effective for inclusive education settings.

ABOUT THE SYSTEM

This educational system is the result of a research-based project aimed at addressing learning barriers faced by deaf and mute students. Our study highlighted the lack of suitable digital tools that cater to their communication needs. Based on our findings, we developed a static website that delivers visual and sign language-supported content. The system’s design reflects principles of inclusive education and offers a practical, affordable solution for schools with limited internet access. This research contributes to the development of accessible technology and sets a foundation for future innovations in special education tools and methodologies.

PROJECT SCOPE

Project Title:

Interactive Static Website for Deaf and Mute Student Learning Support


In-Scope:

Three main learning modules:
- Shapes Learning (with visual aids and signs)
- Currency Recognition (coins and notes)
- Basic Mathematics (addition, subtraction, etc.)


Target Users:

- Deaf and mute students (primary and middle school level)
- Special education teachers and caregivers

Objective:

To design and develop a static web-based educational platform that helps deaf and mute students learn basic concepts such as shapes, currency, and mathematics through sign language animations and visual content.

Out-of-Scope:

- User login or authentication system
- Dynamic data storage or real-time interaction
- Advanced analytics or tracking features

RESEARCH GAP

Despite advancements in digital education, there is still a significant gap in learning tools tailored for deaf and mute students. Most existing platforms are designed for general learners and rely heavily on auditory instructions, making them inaccessible for students with hearing and speech impairments. Additionally, there is a lack of structured educational systems that use sign language and visual elements to teach basic concepts such as shapes, currency, and mathematics. This research identifies the absence of inclusive, visual-based content in mainstream educational tools and aims to bridge this gap by developing a dedicated static website designed specifically for these students.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

Deaf and mute students often face challenges in understanding basic academic concepts due to the lack of accessible and inclusive learning materials. Most traditional education systems and online platforms rely on spoken language and auditory cues, making it difficult for these students to engage and learn effectively. There is a noticeable shortage of educational tools that use sign language and visual content to teach fundamental topics like shapes, currency, and basic mathematics. This creates a barrier to equal learning opportunities and slows down the academic progress of students with hearing and speech impairments.

SOLUTION

To address this issue, we propose a web application specifically designed to support deaf and mute students in learning basic academic concepts through visual and sign language-based methods. The system includes three main learning modules: shapes, currency, and mathematics, each equipped with animations, icons, and sign language guidance. This solution eliminates the need for sound or verbal instructions, allowing students to learn independently at their own pace. The nature of the website ensures easy deployment and accessibility, especially in low-resource environments such as rural schools or homes with limited internet access.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

To develop an interactive learning module for shapes

Design a visually engaging module that teaches basic geometric shapes using animations and sign language, helping deaf and mute students identify and differentiate between common shapes effectively.

To create a currency recognition system using local money visuals

Introduce students to the concept of currency by presenting realistic images of local coins and notes, supported by sign language, to improve their understanding of money handling in real-life situations.

To build a basic mathematics learning platform

Enable students to practice simple mathematical operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division through visual tools and guided sign instructions, promoting independent and confident learning.
